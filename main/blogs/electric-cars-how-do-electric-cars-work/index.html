Electric Cars : How Do Electric Cars work?

By : Charity Prado from US Green Technology

15.1k reads
5 min read
25 August 22

If you're concerned about the life of your Tesla battery, fret not: the company has automobiles that last for 300 to 400 miles. Tesla is at the front of the electric car revolution because their models offer some of the industry's finest battery ranges. Read on to know about how ranges fluctuate across Tesla models, why various vehicles have varied ranges and how long do Tesla batteries last.

On a single charge, the batteries in Tesla vehicles can power the vehicles for an average of 336 miles. The Tesla Model 3 has a range of just 267 miles, whereas the Model S has a range of 405 miles, making the Model 3 the model with the shortest range.

It is possible that the Model 3 will need to be charged once every few days if it is used for daily commuting, but otherwise it will operate admirably. Both variants of the Model S have a substantial driving range, allowing drivers of longer distances to reach their destinations without having to make an excessive number of stops at Superchargers.

Why Do Battery Ranges Vary By Model?

Looking at how long the batteries in each Tesla are expected to last, you may be wondering why the ranges of Tesla's various vehicles are so different from one another. Tesla's vehicle models, much like those produced by traditional manufacturers of gas-powered automobiles, each have characteristics that set them apart from one another and, as a result, influence the amount of time their batteries can maintain their charge. The most essential factors in establishing the exact range of an electric vehicle are the size of its battery and the type of the vehicle.

Battery size

As a rule of thumb, the larger the battery, the greater the amount of energy that can be stored and, as a result, the greater the range. For instance, the battery capacity of the Tesla Model S is 98 kilowatt-hours (kWh), but the battery capacity of the Tesla Model 3 is 80.5 kWh. This makes perfect sense, considering that the Model S has a range that is far greater than that of the Model 3.

Tesla model

Electric automobiles that are able to make more effective use of the energy they have stored will be able to get more mileage. A number of factors, including aerodynamics, the amount of friction that occurs between the tires and the road, the efficiency of the powertrain, and the weight of the vehicle, all contribute. A Tesla model with drivetrain technology (which is often found in newer models) and a low weight will utilize the stored power more effectively than a model having an older drivetrain that is less efficient and a frame that is more cumbersome.

How long do Tesla batteries last?

Elon Musk has said on his Twitter account that the batteries in Tesla vehicles are designed to theoretically survive for 300,000 - 500,000 miles, that is equivalent to 1,500 battery cycles. According to the Department of Transportation, it amounts to around 22 to 37 years for an average automobile driver, and a total of 13,500 miles per year.

It is important to note that this isn't the same distance as the warranties offered by Tesla. Once 100,000-150,000 miles (depending on the model), Tesla will no longer cover the cost of repairs or replacements for your battery if it has degraded beyond a certain point. The company cannot guarantee how well your battery will function after it has been driven that far.

If you drive a Tesla vehicle 300,000 - 500,000 miles on a single battery, you will probably be dealing with a battery that is quite worn out. The cost of changing the battery in your Tesla might be difficult to estimate, but you don't have to wait until you've racked up a certain amount of miles before doing so. Musk states that it may cost between $5,000 & $7,000 to replace a single module in the same tweet that was stated above. However, you should bear in mind that the Tesla battery pack has numerous modules, so it would not be cost efficient to replace your whole battery.

The Bottom Line

We hope by the end of this article you have got a fair idea of how long do Tesla batteries last and the different factors that play an imperative role in the life of your Tesla battery. It will be wise for you to know beforehand about the battery life for each Tesla car and the battery replacement cost before you make any purchase.
